<p><em>How to create emotions for an emotionless character&nbsp;was the challenge for this project.&nbsp; Connor is a character&nbsp;with a mission in mind, and throughout this singular vision, things can change as well as questions will rise to challenge this mission.</em></p>



<p>I made an effort to “become” Connor by play every instrument and every note of the score somehow or another.&nbsp; For the colors of the score, I relied heavily on vintage and custom synthesizers, modular synths, custom instruments as well as modified western instruments&nbsp;such as Violin and Violas. One of these custom instruments is a&nbsp;20 Foot Guitar&nbsp;built&nbsp;via inner working of an android in mind.&nbsp; With this instrument mostly making sub frequencies, the mechanism had to be designed carefully and strategically for the creation of the sound and the methods of capturing this sound.&nbsp; Contact microphones and amplifiers were used prominently&nbsp;for this Guitar with an electroacoustic&nbsp;philosophy in mind.&nbsp; The String family for this project, more specifically the&nbsp;Violin and Violas, were strategically rebuilt. The strings, bridge, and bowing methods were explored to capture the interesting&nbsp;colors of the project. Also, unplugged electric Violin, Viola, and Cello were recorded to create a cold unfiltered sound.&nbsp;The Resonant Garden, and&nbsp;Mescaline&nbsp;build by the amazing&nbsp;FOLKTEK group were used prominently for some of the melodies as well as creating the pads and the atmospheres of the score.&nbsp; For the main motif of Connor,&nbsp;I used a&nbsp;Vintage Moog Voyager&nbsp;to create a sense of weight as well as simplicity&nbsp;for the theme.</p>
